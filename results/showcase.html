The whole study

One arc, from scaling to a cost result

Decentralisation removes the coordinator bottleneck; the risk is that self-selection rests on a miscalibrated self-report; a track-record correction fixes it; and the two wrappers turn the mechanism into frontier quality at a fraction of the cost.

Task wrapper · H11 · real agents

The contract lifts the weak model to the frontier

bare prompt Haiku 4.5 Sonnet 5 Opus 4.8

Completion on the 8-task expert tier. Under a bare prompt, completion climbs with capability (Haiku 0.875, Sonnet and Opus 1.00). The task wrapper names the exact edge case the weak model missed and lifts Haiku to 1.00, leaving the already-saturated models unchanged: the contract helps most where the model is weakest.

Bars show completion (fraction of tasks fully passing). Haiku bare carries a wide interval on a small sample: 0.875, bootstrap 95% CI [0.69, 1.00].

Agent wrapper · H12 · real agents

Frontier completion, at a fraction of the cost

always-frontier routed · wrapped routed · bare, per-task track record routed · bare, coarse track record

Completion against relative cost for four strategies on the expert tier. Always using the frontier model reaches 1.00 at full cost. Routing over task-wrapped tasks matches it (1.00) at about 1/47th of the cost. Routing over bare tasks with only a coarse track record loses completion (0.875) — the weak model's overconfident bid on the hardest task clears the barrier and fails. Give the router a per-task track record and it escalates exactly that task, recovering 1.00 at ≈1/20th cost.

Cost is relative to always-frontier (log axis), priced at representative economy-to-premium tiers; the multiples illustrate the mechanism, not a live quote.

Project · H11 + H12 · real agents

A contract is the precondition for a swarm to build software

bare spec Haiku 4.5 Sonnet 5 Opus 4.8

Modules passing (of 5) on the miniquery project, a five-module toolkit with a dependency graph. Under a loose spec every model, including the frontier one, delivers a divergent interface and no working project (0 completion). The interface contract makes all three deliver a conforming project. The router then assembles a working cross-model project from task-wrapped modules at ≈1/39th of always-frontier cost; the bare assembly fails at any price.

Mechanism · H7 → H8 · simulation

Why it works: a track record fixes miscalibration

raw self-report auction reliability-corrected

Task completion under the worst injected overconfidence. A raw self-report auction selects well-fitted but not competent agents, and completion falls to 0.37. Discounting each bid by the agent's observable track record — no privileged information — recovers it to 0.76 (Holm-corrected p < 0.001, 20 seeds). This is the same correction the agent wrapper uses to route, and the same failure the bare router shows on real agents.

Honesty & reproduction

What this is, and is not

The synthetic results are full-protocol scale (20 seeds, agent counts to 10,000) and deterministic from seeds. The real-agent results (the ladder, the project, the cost multiples) are a small but honest sample — one to two agents per cell — so the direction is clear and the intervals are wide; hardening to a powered estimate is future work. Prices are representative tiers, not a live quote. Against a full-information optimum the quality claim is reported as not clearing its margin. Reproduce the synthetic arc with cta reproduce-all and the real-agent tiers with python -m pilot_tasks.ladder and python -m pilot_tasks.project.
