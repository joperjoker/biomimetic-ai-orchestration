# Changelog

All notable changes to this project are recorded in this file. The format follows the spirit of Keep a Changelog, and the project aims to follow semantic versioning once a first release is cut.

## [Unreleased]

### Added

- Streaming task arrival (P3.3). `TemporalConfig.arrival_span` staggers task arrival evenly over that many rounds instead of all at round 0 (span 0 preserves the exact batch behaviour); a task is PENDING until it arrives, and its stall and barrier annealing are measured from its own arrival. `harness.streaming_arrival` extends H5 to this non-stationary load: on the stall-prone scenario, annealing on resolves every feasible task with a bounded stall (max stall about 3, unmet 0) while annealing off leaves them stalled to the horizon (max stall 300, unmet 1.0). Wired into `autorun` and the H5 verdict row; new `tests/test_streaming.py`, including a batch-arrival regression guard.
- Strategic adversary demoted by the track record (P3.2). `harness.strategic_adversary` runs sequential batches, updating each winner's record from its realised outcomes, so an agent that inflates its self-report to win bids while being incompetent is progressively demoted under reliability-weighted selection: its share of won tasks falls from about 0.22 in the first round to near zero by the last, with no anticipation of the gaming needed. A light experiment that reuses the batch engine round by round (no core-engine change, no model calls); wired into `autorun` and a paper supporting-analysis paragraph. New `tests/test_strategic_adversary.py`.
- Calibration grounded in the measured spread (P2.1). `realism.with_fitted_miscalibration` draws each agent's calibration bias and noise from the measured MarketBench archetype mixture instead of one injected overconfidence, and `harness.fitted_calibration_recovery` repeats the raw-versus-reliability completion comparison on that population. The track-record correction still recovers completion by about 0.48 (0.37 to 0.85 at 20 seeds), so the H8 result is not an artefact of the injected bias shape. Wired into `autorun` and a paper robustness sentence; new tests in `tests/test_realism.py` and `tests/test_fitted_recovery.py`.
- Product proof of concept (P2.6). A runnable `examples/poc` (`python -m examples.poc`) demonstrates the mechanism offline and deterministically: a small fleet self-selects tasks through the calibrated, gated bid, the integrity gate stops the out-of-scope actions of adversarial agents, and the coordinator cost is reported (central per-node bill against the decentralised one). `run_poc` returns a summary; `tests/test_poc.py` covers the demonstration and determinism. Referenced from the paper's commercialisation discussion and `REPRODUCE.md`.
- Latency-quality Pareto frontier (P2.2). The Binding Energy's latency term now carries a swept exponent (`run_batch(..., latency_weight=...)`, default 1.0 preserving current behaviour): 0 ignores latency and maximises quality, larger values favour faster agents. New `harness.pareto_sweep` and `harness.pareto_front` trace the non-dominated frontier of mean realised quality against mean winner latency (quality about 0.91 at latency about 1.0 down to about 0.87 at about 0.65). Wired into `autorun` (a `pareto_latency_quality.svg` figure and summary block), the dashboard, and a paper paragraph framing it as the speed side of the product dial next to the cost model. New `tests/test_latency_weight.py` and `tests/test_pareto.py`.
- Heterogeneous specialist routing (H10). A new `src/cta/routing.py` and `harness.routing_experiment` run a job of role-specific subtasks over role-specific specialists and measure routing accuracy (the fraction of won subtasks that go to a same-domain specialist). Finding: the activation barrier holds routing at 1.00 at every observability level, far above the 0.25 chance floor for four roles, because an ill-matched agent never fires; without the barrier, agents fire on whatever they observe and routing collapses to 0.47 under tight observability, recovering to 0.99 as observability widens, at a coverage cost (about 29 versus 61 won) that activation-energy annealing (H5) later reclaims. This gives the activation barrier the quality role the batch ablation (P2.4) could not surface, since that ablation has no observability or time axis. Wired into `autorun` (a `specialist_routing.svg` figure and a `routing` summary block), the dashboard, and the paper (H10 in the hypotheses, falsification, verdict table, RQ10, a discussion paragraph, and the abstract). New `tests/test_routing.py` and `tests/test_routing_experiment.py`. One hundred and two tests pass; `ruff` clean. Also adds a single `cta reproduce-all` entry point.
- Fairness, scale, biomimicry evidence and the commercial story (a full pass over the next-experiments plan). An information-bounded central baseline (`baselines.run_central_bounded`) that allocates from self-reports and a reliability table degraded by a staleness parameter, giving the fair, beatable form of the coordination question as H9: CTA is level with it when the table is fresh (about 0.88 against 0.91) and pulls ahead as it goes stale (about 0.88 against 0.64, Holm-corrected p about 0.03), because a decentralised agent never pays the batch synchronisation lag. The scaling curve is fitted (`stats.fit_scaling`) and extended to ten thousand agents behind a central load-only fast path (`baselines.coordinator_cost`): CTA's growth exponent is 0.0 [0.0, 0.0] against the central 2.0 [2.0, 2.0], with the central peak load reaching eighty million. Headline means gain percentile bootstrap confidence intervals (`stats.bootstrap_ci`) and a power note (`stats.min_seeds`) reporting the seeds a comparison needs versus those run. The raw per-seed, per-condition rows are released as a flat CSV with a data dictionary (`src/cta/dataset.py`, `results/dataset/runs.csv`, `cta dataset`). A biomimicry ablation (`harness.biomimicry_ablation`, `report.ablation_attribution`) isolates the mechanisms: the integrity gate cuts out-of-scope violations from twenty to two (a 90 per cent reduction, Holm-significant), while the activation barrier is quality-neutral in the batch regime and is reported as such. A token and dollar cost model (`src/cta/cost.py`) turns the pair-evaluation count into money: at ten thousand agents the central bill is about 144,000 USD per allocation round against about 576 USD decentralised, a roughly 250 times saving whose quadratic-versus-flat shape is structural. A single `cta reproduce-all` regenerates every result, figure and the dataset from seeds. Repository hygiene (dead stub directories removed, `docs/architecture.md` de-staled), a validity bullet for the added comparisons, and the executable plan (`docs/next_experiments.md`, including the planned H10 specialist-routing study) are added. Ninety-seven tests pass; `ruff` clean.
- Live pilot with real coding agents (real budget spend). Three Claude Code subagents each stated a confidence and solved thirteen coding micro-tasks with validated hidden edge-case tests (`pilot_tasks/`), giving 39 real self-report-versus-outcome pairs. The agent solved every task at mean confidence about 0.92, so it is underconfident (overconfidence gap about minus 0.08, Brier 0.008), matching MarketBench's finding that the Claude models are well calibrated to underconfident. This confirms on real data that a coding agent's self-reports are miscalibrated (the study's premise) and that the sign is model-dependent, which is why the correction reweights by an observable track record. Adds the task suite and reference validator (`pilot_tasks/suite.py`), the scorer and analyser (`pilot_tasks/score_submissions.py`, `analyse.py`), the committed submissions and data (`results/live_pilot/`), a real reliability diagram (`results/figures/reliability_live.svg`, on the dashboard), `docs/live_pilot.md`, a paper paragraph, and `tests/test_live_pilot.py`. Eighty-one tests pass; `ruff` clean.
- Realistic fleet grounded in measured LLM calibration, and the reliability diagram. `src/cta/realism.py` adds three calibration archetypes (overconfident, calibrated, underconfident) parameterised from the stated-versus-realised gaps MarketBench reports for current coding models on SWE-bench Lite (overconfidence bias about +0.15, 0, -0.16), a `generate_fleet` mixing them, and a `FleetClient` whose self-report is a success estimate as in the auction setting. The fleet runs through the same pilot pipeline (with `run_pilot` gaining a `selection` mode and an imperfect gate consistent with the batch engine). On this realistic fleet the track-record correction still recovers completion (about 0.06 to 0.08), improves the winners' Brier score, and the gate still cuts violations by about 0.8; a fleet-composition sweep keeps the recovery positive from an all-calibrated to an all-overconfident fleet. The missing calibration visualisation is added: `engine.reliability_bins` plus a reliability diagram (predicted versus realised success with the diagonal) and a fleet-mix figure. Wired into `autorun`, the dashboard, and the paper (a realistic-fleet paragraph and an external-validity note). New `tests/test_realism.py`. Seventy-eight tests pass; `ruff` clean.
- Live-pilot scaffold (Stage 2), runnable with a mock client and no model calls. `src/cta/pilot.py` now defines a `PilotClient` seam with `assess` (the agent's self-reported compatibility, E13) and `perform` (realised quality and a scope check), a deterministic `MockClient` that drives the whole pipeline end to end, and a `ClaudeAgentClient` stub that marks where a live Claude Code subagent plugs in and raises until implemented, so the scaffold cannot incur cost by accident. `run_pilot` reuses the framework's eligibility, activation barrier, reliability-weighted selection (with an online track record) and the Rejection Gate, and reports the same calibration measures (overconfidence gap, Brier, ECE, integrity violations). A `cta pilot` command runs it. New `tests/test_pilot.py`. Seventy-three tests pass; `ruff` clean.
- Pure-quality selection mode and the H2 gap decomposition. A `quality` selection mode ranks on the competence-weighted self-report without the latency term (`c_hat * R`, Binding Energy minus the `/ L`), so the quality shortfall in H2 can be attributed to its cause. `harness.h2_decomposition` reports the deployed cost-aware quality, the quality-first quality, and the full-information optimum, splitting the gap into a latency (cost-for-speed) component and a competence-proxy component. The result: a quality-first CTA reaches about 0.93, within the margin of the optimum, so about three quarters of the gap is the deliberate quality-for-latency tradeoff and only about a quarter is the noisy track record. `autorun` gains the `h2_decomposition.svg` figure and summary block, the dashboard shows it, and the paper's H2 row and Discussion carry the decomposition. Sixty-nine tests pass; `ruff` clean.
- Generalisability and sensitivity pass, with a visual dashboard. A second, structurally different generator family (`latent`: continuous latent directions, smooth cosine compatibility, no discrete skill gate) is added alongside the original `domains` family and threaded through `CellParams.family`. The population-dependent hypotheses are re-run under it: H4, H7, and H8 hold under both families and H2 does not under either, evidence the findings track the mechanism rather than the synthetic structure. Sensitivity sweeps replace single points with bands: `recovery_vs_spread` (the correction's recovery rises with competence spread, about 0.05 to 0.41), `reduction_vs_recall` (the gate's violation reduction rises with detection recall, about 0.57 to 1.0), and a `recovery_surface` over overconfidence bias by competence spread (horizontal bands, so the gap is spread-driven, not bias-driven). New pure-SVG `heatmap` and `bar_chart` renderers, and four new figures. A self-contained HTML dashboard (`cta dashboard`, also written by `autorun`) inlines every figure with the verdicts and the cross-generator comparison. The paper's 2.7 generalisability commitment is fulfilled and section 3 gains a robustness table and a sensitivity paragraph. Sixty-eight tests pass; `ruff` clean.
- Calibration study deepened (Tier 2). The engine now reports the Brier score and the expected calibration error (ECE) of the winners' self-reports against their binary success (`engine._brier_ece`), so H7 quantifies miscalibration in the standard forecasting language (about 0.26 for both under overconfidence) rather than only the overconfidence gap. A new `harness.track_record_sweep` varies the length of the reliability record from 2 to 40 prior attempts and shows the correction is cheap in data: a two-attempt record already recovers most of the completion gap, and both the recovery and the winners' calibration improve with a longer record. `cta autorun` gains the `track_record_recovery.svg` figure and the `track_record` summary block, and the calibration sweep records the Brier and ECE per bias. The paper's metrics (2.4), H7 row, a supporting-analysis paragraph, and `docs/measures.md` are updated. Sixty-two tests pass; `ruff` clean.
- Statistical and baseline rigour (Tier 1). The Holm-Bonferroni multiple-comparison correction that section 2.6 promised is now actually applied: `evaluate` collects the family of p-value tests (H2, H8), corrects them via `holm_over_hypotheses`, records `p_holm` and `significant_holm`, and H8's verdict rests on the corrected significance. A fair full-information baseline `central_best` (each task to the agent maximising expected realised quality, agent reuse allowed) replaces the handicapped one-to-one Hungarian optimum as the reference for H2 and H6, so CTA no longer spuriously beats the optimum. Agents now carry an informative track record (successes drawn from their true capability), so reliability `R` is a real competence signal rather than a uniform constant; this is the realistic setting in which reliability-weighted selection can function. Consequence, reported honestly: against the fair optimum CTA reaches about 94 per cent of the optimum's quality and is level with pull-based, so H2 no longer clears the 5 per cent margin and is not supported; the shortfall is the cost-awareness of Binding Energy plus a noisy competence proxy. H6 likewise sits just below the fair optimum. The paper's baseline (2.3), H2, H6, Results, and Discussion are updated to match.
- Rigour pass and a temporal engine (after a critical review of the calibration draft). The Rejection Gate is now modelled as an imperfect detector (`GateConfig.scope_recall`), so the safety result H4 is a measured reduction (about 83 per cent at recall 0.9) rather than a tautological zero, and H7 no longer claims the overconfidence gap grows with the injected bias (it is the structural fit-versus-competence gap). New `src/cta/temporal.py`, a round-based engine with agents that hold work over time, task stalls, and activation-energy annealing (E14), which makes allocation latency, throughput, starvation, and annealing into real measured quantities. H5 is now tested by the temporal engine (`harness.annealing_curve`): without annealing feasible tasks are never claimed, with a positive rate they resolve at bounded stall, and the maximum stall falls as the rate rises. `harness.temporal_metrics` reports latency and throughput; `cta autorun` gains the `annealing_stall.svg` figure and the `annealing` and `temporal` summary blocks. The paper's H4, H5, and H7 statements, Results, Discussion, and architecture, plus `docs/measures.md`, are updated. Sixty tests pass; `ruff` clean.

- Measurable model (`docs/measures.md`) and the task wrapper: every quantity is given a concrete, measurable definition. Compatibility `c` (from an agent's role, skills, and prompt) replaces the abstract signal `S`, and the activation barrier is now on compatibility (`c >= Ea`) with the Binding Energy `B = c x C_tilde / L` used only to rank the willing agents. Compatibility aggregates measurable sub-scores (semantic match, skill coverage, scope fit) and can be calibrated to predict realised success. Ground-truth quality, cost, complexity, and the activation energy are all defined operationally. The paper (sections 1 and 2.2), theory, glossary, and claude.md are reconciled, and the stale Supabase references are replaced with the self-contained store.
- Reference verification: confirmed all nine references online, corrected the Kuhn issue number to 2(1-2), added the missing in-text citation for Dias et al. (2006), added DOIs for Kuhn, Gerkey, Dorigo, and Smith, and cleared the now-confirmed Recursive Language Models verification flags.
- Principal-level review (AI, mathematics, and science) with judged revisions: added three principal personas to the Consortium; added a decentralised pull-based baseline to isolate CTA's mechanisms from decentralisation; made agent heterogeneity a first-class variable with RQ6 and H6; added multiple-comparison control, a power note, and explicit falsification criteria; sharpened the novelty statement; and documented that `L` is a normalised relative cost so the absolute `Ea` is interpretable.
